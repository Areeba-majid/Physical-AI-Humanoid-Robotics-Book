# Chapter 4: Humanoid Robotics

This chapter explores the fascinating world of humanoid robotics, covering the design, mechanics, and control systems that enable robots to mimic human form and behavior.

## Topics

- Anatomy of Humanoid Robots
- Biomechanics and Movement
- Balance and Locomotion
- Human-Robot Interaction
- Applications and Future Trends

Humanoid robots represent one of the most ambitious goals in robotics, attempting to replicate the form and function of human beings to better interact with human-designed environments and social structures.

## Anatomy of Humanoid Robots

Humanoid robots typically feature:
- Head with sensors for perception
- Torso with actuated joints
- Two arms with manipulator hands
- Two legs for locomotion
- Complex control systems
